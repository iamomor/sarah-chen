Austin's real estate market enters the summer of 2026 in a state of careful equilibrium. The frenzied pace of 2022–2024 has given way to a more measured environment — one that rewards prepared buyers and strategically positioned sellers in equal measure.

Here is what the current data tells us, and what it means for you.

## Where Inventory Stands Right Now

Active listings across Greater Austin climbed to approximately **7,400 units** in April 2026, up 11% year-over-year. At the luxury tier — homes priced above $1.5M — inventory has expanded more significantly, reaching a 5.2-month supply. That is the most breathing room buyers have had in this segment since early 2019.

Below the $900K threshold, however, the story is different. Months of supply in that range remain compressed at just **2.1 months**, meaning well-priced homes are still moving fast. Entry-level and mid-range properties in desirable school districts are attracting competitive offer situations, while luxury estates are seeing longer negotiating windows open up.

**What this means for buyers:** If you have been waiting for the right moment to purchase in a premium Austin neighborhood, the luxury sector offers the best conditions in years. Sellers are willing to negotiate on price, closing costs, and repair credits in a way that was nearly impossible eighteen months ago.

**What this means for sellers:** Correct pricing from day one is non-negotiable. Properties that come to market overpriced by even 4–5% are sitting — sometimes for 60+ days — before requiring a reduction. Work with an advisor who understands your neighborhood's micro-pricing dynamics, not just county-wide averages.

## Median Price Trends

The Austin-Round Rock MSA median sold price landed at **$487,000** in April 2026 — down approximately 3.2% from April 2025, but showing month-over-month stabilization since February. Luxury is holding far better than the overall market suggests:

- **Westlake (Eanes ISD):** Median closed price of $2.1M, with a price-per-square-foot of **$815**, essentially flat year-over-year.
- **Tarrytown:** Median of $1.74M, with the fastest absorption in the city at an average of **17 days on market** for properly staged, correctly priced homes.
- **Hyde Park:** Steady appreciation of 4.2% annually on historic architecture, driven by limited buildable land within the Local Historic District boundaries.

Interest rates have stabilized in the **6.0–6.4% range** for conventional 30-year mortgages, with jumbo products (above $806,500) running slightly tighter at 6.1–6.5%. Rate volatility has decreased substantially compared to 2025, and this predictability is drawing sidelined buyers back into the market.

## Neighborhood Spotlight

### Tarrytown

Tarrytown continues to defy broader market softness. Its combination of walkability, mature-canopied streets, access to Lake Austin boat ramps, and Casis Elementary School zoning creates a demand floor that other Austin zip codes simply cannot replicate. New listings in Tarrytown receive, on average, 3.7 showing requests within the first 48 hours — a figure that has not materially changed even as broader inventory expanded.

### Westlake

Westlake's appeal among high-income tech and finance executives has only deepened. With Amazon, Apple, and Tesla maintaining and expanding their Austin footprints, the steady stream of six-figure relocation buyers continues. Luxury new construction in the Rollingwood and Westlake Hills corridor is selling at pre-completion in several cases.

## A Note on Timing

The late spring and summer window (May through August) historically produces Austin's highest transaction volume. For sellers, listing before school breaks begin in late May captures families who want to close and settle before the new academic year. For buyers, the window between June and August often surfaces motivated sellers who need to transact before fall.

## Key Takeaways

- Luxury inventory is expanding — buyers have leverage they have not had since 2019
- Sub-$900K homes are still highly competitive with limited supply
- Tarrytown and Westlake are outperforming the broader MSA on both price retention and velocity
- Rate stability is improving buyer confidence and transaction volume
