Austin has dozens of neighborhoods. Only one consistently defies broader market softness, maintains sub-20-day absorption rates through multiple economic cycles, and commands the highest price-per-square-foot among established central neighborhoods. That neighborhood is Tarrytown.

This is not sentiment. It is supply and demand, expressed over decades.

## The Supply Problem (That Sellers Love)

Tarrytown occupies roughly 1.2 square miles of land bounded by MoPac Expressway to the west, Lake Austin Boulevard to the south, and Windsor Road to the north. It is **geographically constrained** — there is no room to expand the supply of land.

Combined with strict deed restrictions that limit teardown-and-rebuild density in much of the neighborhood, the total available housing stock in Tarrytown barely changes from year to year. As of May 2026, there are fewer than **22 active single-family listings** in the entire neighborhood. At the current absorption rate, that represents less than 2 months of inventory — a deeply undersupplied market by any definition.

When supply is structurally capped and demand continues to grow, price floors hold even when broader market conditions soften.

## Three Demand Drivers That Do Not Go Away

### 1. Lake Austin Access

Tarrytown sits adjacent to Lake Austin, offering private dock access, kayak put-in points, and one of the most scenic recreational corridors in Central Texas. In a landlocked city with limited waterfront property, proximity to the lake adds a genuine and permanent premium. Properties with confirmed lake access or views regularly close at **15–25% above neighborhood medians**.

### 2. Casis Elementary and the Eanes / Austin ISD Line

School district zoning is one of the most reliable predictors of residential demand in any American market, and Tarrytown is no exception. Homes zoned for **Casis Elementary School** — consistently ranked among the top elementary schools in Austin ISD — command a measurable buyer premium.

Families with children relocating to Austin from other metros consistently identify Casis Elementary as a key decision variable. This creates a buyer pool that is motivated, pre-qualified, and frequently willing to move quickly when the right property appears.

### 3. Ten-Minute Downtown Access Without Urban Density

The defining tension in Austin real estate is the tradeoff between proximity to downtown and the quality of the residential experience. Most close-in neighborhoods sacrifice quiet streets and mature tree canopy for urban walkability. Tarrytown refuses the tradeoff.

Its location places residents within **8–12 minutes of downtown Austin** — and of the city's major employment corridors — without the noise, density, or lack of green space that characterize urban neighborhoods. This combination is rare and deeply difficult to replicate.

## What the Numbers Show in 2026

- **Average days on market:** 17 (city average: 38)
- **List-to-sale price ratio:** 98.4% — properties rarely require deep discounting
- **Year-over-year price change:** +3.1% in April 2026, outperforming the broader Austin MSA's -3.2%
- **Price per square foot:** $875–$950 for updated single-family homes

These figures are not flukes. They are consistent across 2021, 2022, 2023, 2024, and 2025. The neighborhood performs because its fundamentals are durable, not because it caught a wave at the right moment.

## What This Means If You Are Considering a Purchase or Sale

For **sellers**: Tarrytown properties priced within 2–3% of current market comps will transact quickly. Buyers know what they want here, and they come prepared. Over-pricing by 5%+ is the one reliable way to sit on the market longer than necessary in this zip code.

For **buyers**: Tarrytown does not reward waiting. Watching from the sidelines while "seeing if prices drop further" has historically been a losing strategy in this specific market. When a well-maintained property in Tarrytown appears at fair market value, it will be gone before most buyers have completed their first walkthrough.
